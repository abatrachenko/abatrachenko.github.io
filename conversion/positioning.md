## 1. ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)

**Role:** VP Marketing · Director of Marketing · VP E-commerce · Director of E-commerce.
One coherent cluster — all growth-P&L owners at senior-enough tier to sign $6.4K–$12.8K/month without a procurement committee.

**Company:** Enterprise e-commerce brands, $50M+ annual revenue. Fashion, DTC, retail, consumer brands, specialty commerce.
Not for brands under $50M, not for early-stage DTC, not for pure-SaaS or services companies.

**Industry anchor:** Enterprise e-commerce specialist. The logo cluster that proves this — Adidas, Samsung, J.Crew, Madewell — is the strongest single proof set in the client portfolio. Ethereum, Alchemy, HBS, KPN are "also trusted by" not anchor clients.

**Headline pain (primary trigger):** Stalled or decelerating organic growth. Team plateau, agency disappointment, or paid CAC rising past sustainable levels — any of these paths lead the buyer to a senior SEO consultant.

**Secondary triggers** (woven into below-the-fold copy):
- **Capability gap in-house** — "Our team needs senior direction on what to prioritize."
- **Growth acceleration** — "Just raised / new mandate / board wants 10x organic."

**Budget authority:** Can sign $6.4K–$12.8K/month without committee. Has a discretionary consulting budget or a line for external senior advisors.

## 3. Differentiator triangle

Three pillars. Each neutralizes a distinct alternative the buyer is considering.

### Pillar 1 — Embedded senior operator (vs. agencies)
Direct work with the senior who owns the strategy. No account managers, no juniors running playbooks, no retainer markup.

### Pillar 2 — Enterprise-scale track record (vs. smaller consultants)
$700M+ in organic revenue generated. Engagements with brands the size of Adidas, Samsung, J.Crew, Madewell. Experience operating inside 10K+ URL inventories, 1000+ person orgs, and the interlock between engineering / content / marketing that enterprise SEO actually requires.

### Pillar 3 — GEO + SEO capability (vs. yesterday's SEO consultants)
Few SEO operators work at senior level across both traditional search and AI-powered search. Combines classical SEO execution with emerging GEO strategy.

*The Reach LLM advisory shows up as a credential in the bio section and optionally in one FAQ entry about GEO tooling — not as a headline pillar and not as a product pitch. Reach is one of several tools that may be recommended depending on the client's stack.*

## 5. Offer structure

### Service
Fractional SEO + GEO leadership, embedded in the client's team. Ongoing monthly engagement (not project-based).

### Tiers (preserved from current site)
- **32 hrs/mo** — $6,400/mo — strategic guidance and oversight
- **48 hrs/mo** — $9,600/mo — full strategic integration *(previously "Most Popular" badged — badge removed)*
- **64 hrs/mo** — $12,800/mo — dedicated fractional leadership

### Commitment
3-month minimum. Monthly invoicing, due 15th of the following month.

### Availability signal (preserved)
The "Hours Available This Month: X of 160" progress bar is a real scarcity signal the consultant updates manually. Stays on the site.

### CTA strategy — two-layer, staged launch

**Primary CTA (above the fold + first 2–3 sections):** Email capture at first-touch.

The target state is a lead magnet — **GEO Readiness Framework** (vendor-neutral, "How visible is your brand in ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews?"). Framework production is in flight as a parallel track (2026-04-23 decision).

Launch in two stages:

- **Stage 1 (rebuild ships first):** interim newsletter signup — *"Organic growth patterns for enterprise e-commerce. Every other Tuesday."* Captures email, builds the list, no production blocker on the rebuild.
- **Stage 2 (when framework is ready):** swap the primary CTA to the framework download. Newsletter remains as a secondary capture point in the footer. Everyone who signed up in Stage 1 gets the framework delivered to their inbox as the first nurture email.

**Secondary CTA (after proof + case studies, mid-bottom of page):**
*"Book a strategy call"* (or *"Book a working session"*) → Calendly. No "free" in the label.

All CTAs use the same wording. Consolidate from the current 8 placements to 2–3 deliberate ones.

### GEO implementation
Integrated into whatever an engagement needs, not a separate productized service pillar. If a client wants AI search capability built out, it happens inside the regular hourly allocation. Reach LLM may be recommended; other tools may be recommended; the choice depends on the client's stack, not on vendor alignment.

## 6. Voice

**Style: B + E blend** — technical-plainspoken (Julian Shapiro / Benji Kay) combined with research-led (data-forward, careful qualifiers). Equal weights.

**Voice is:**
- Specific, concrete, number-forward
- Calm and declarative — no exclamation points, no hype
- Qualifier-aware ("most," "often," "depending on") — no grand universal claims
- Operator-voiced — sounds like someone describing work they've done, not a marketer pitching it

**Voice is NOT:**
- Listicle teases ("3 things," "5 mistakes," "the one question...")
- Hook kickers ("Here's what...," "Here's how...")
- LinkedIn / Twitter content-marketing patterns
- First-person emotional appeals or story-arcs
- Exclamation points, rhetorical questions as openers, or cliffhanger structure

**Calibration sample (right):**
> Organic revenue at enterprise scale compounds when the strategy is senior and execution is embedded. Most brands lose it in handoffs — between agency and team, between strategist and implementer, between SEO and the rest of the marketing stack.

**Calibration sample (wrong — don't write this):**
> Most SEO audits miss the three things that actually move revenue. Here's what I check first.

## 7. Anti-positioning

Three explicit boundaries. Stated on the site (not just implied) to filter inbound and signal fit.

### 1. Not an agency
No account managers. No juniors running playbooks. No retainer factory. Client works directly with the senior who owns the strategy.

### 2. Not for brands under $50M revenue (and not for early-stage DTC)
The playbook and the pricing are built for enterprise-scale orgs. Smaller brands are better served by different operators.

### 3. Not a generalist marketing consultant
Scope is SEO + GEO. No paid media, no brand strategy, no growth marketing outside of organic search.
