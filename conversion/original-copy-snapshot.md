## Results (case studies)

### Adidas — Enterprise Sports Retailer (12 mo)
- **Metric:** +240K Daily Unique Clicks
- **Challenge:** Fully reliant on short-tail traffic to lower funnel pages with limited visibility in informational searches.
- **Solution:** Launched and populated the Adidas blog to target long-tail keywords and establish authority in fitness and lifestyle content.
- **Result:** Generated an additional 6 million impressions and 240K unique clicks per day.

### J.Crew — Heritage Fashion Retailer (9 mo)
- **Metric:** +95% Organic Clicks
- **Challenge:** Zero long tail commercial keyword visibility — traffic was limited to brand terms and short-tail queries.
- **Solution:** Created and indexed medium and long tail product landing pages to capture granular, high-intent search queries.
- **Result:** 95% increase in organic clicks over 9 months, unlocking a new revenue channel from previously untapped demand.

### Alchemy — Web3 Infrastructure Platform (24 mo)
- **Metric:** +315% Organic Clicks

- **Solution:** Reworked facets, got them indexed and optimized to capture previously untapped search demand.
- **Result:** 315% increase in organic clicks in 24 months, unlocking significant growth from improved faceted navigation.

## Items removed during stabilization (P0)

These elements were in the original site but were removed as trust hazards or cleanup during P0 stabilization (PR #72 merged 2026-04-23):

- Fabricated social-proof notifications (rotating pop-ups: "Someone in California just booked a call 2 minutes ago" etc. — all hardcoded fiction, never rendered because the JS crashed before they ran)
- Broken exit-intent popup code referencing DOM elements that didn't exist (caused `TypeError` on every page load)
- Placeholder Google Ads conversion ID (`send_to: 'G-XXXXXXXXXX/CONVERSION_ID'` — never tracked anything)
- `<meta name="keywords">` tag (deprecated 2009+)
- `aggregateRating` in JSON-LD (claimed 5 stars / 3 reviews — risks Google manual action)
- "Most Popular" badge on the $9,600 tier (no sales data to back the claim)
- `h4` inside about-callouts and case-study headers (caused heading-order violation under `h2` parents — moved to `h3`)

All of the above are removed deliberately and should not be restored. See `tasks/audit-2026-04-23/AUDIT.md` for full audit and rationale.
