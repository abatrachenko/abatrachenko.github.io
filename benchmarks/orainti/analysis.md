# Orainti

**URL:** https://www.orainti.com
**Captured:** 2026-04-23
**Category:** Solo-expert SEO consultancy (Aleyda Solis) — closest positioning comp to resonanceseo.com

## Why this site

Orainti is the clearest like-for-like reference we have: a senior SEO consultant selling direct-to-operator engagements to enterprise brands, priced off contact, with a single-operator narrative dressed up in boutique-firm framing. Same shape of ICP, same trust structure (named clients + named testimonials), same anti-agency posture. Reading Orainti tells us what the category default looks like — and where there's room to be sharper.

## Hero

- **Headline approach:** provocative-question. H1 reads *"How can Orainti help you?"* — soft, service-first, pivots the page toward the visitor. Not a positioning claim; a conversation opener.
- **Subhead strategy:** expand-promise + category-name. *"Results-driven SEO Consultancy to achieve your business goals."* Eight words of payload, zero specifics (no vertical, no outcome number, no buyer role).
- **CTA:** *"GET IN TOUCH"* — uppercase, solid blue button, immediately below the subhead. Repeated after every major section (at least 4 instances down the page). One CTA phrase, one destination (contact form). No secondary CTA, no email capture, no lead magnet.
- **Supporting visual:** blurred stage photo of Aleyda Solis speaking at a conference. Reads as credential-by-visual — authority without a logo reel above the fold.

## Proof

- **Social proof format:** four named testimonials with full name + title + company, no logo strip on the homepage. Companies surfaced: **Turo, Trade Republic, Krisp, Sniffspot**. One quote carries a hard number — *"Since working with Aleyda for a year we have seen our SEO traffic grow 500%"* (David Adams, Sniffspot). The others are relational ("extension of my internal team," "ultimate expert").
- **Placement:** mid-to-lower page under the *"What Clients Say"* section. Hero has no proof — only the speaking-photo.
- **What makes it credible:** named humans with verifiable titles at recognizable companies, plus one quantified outcome. What's missing (deliberately or otherwise): a client logo cluster, career-cumulative revenue numbers, tenure / years-in-practice stat, press mentions. Proof is entirely relational, not scale-quantified.

## Offer structure

Six service cards with icons, single-sentence descriptions, all on the homepage:

1. International and Multilingual SEO
2. Ecommerce and SaaS SEO
3. SEO Audits and Ongoing Processes
4. Enterprise and Startup SEO Strategy
5. In-House Team SEO Training
6. On-demand SEO Advice

Packaged as a capability menu, not as engagement tiers. No hours-per-month structure, no commitment terms, no retainer sizing on the public site.

**Pricing visibility:** none. "Contact" gate. No price anchor, no availability signal, no minimum engagement stated.

## FAQ / objections

No dedicated FAQ section on the homepage. Objection handling happens implicitly in the *"Why Orainti?"* block — three differentiators framed as **"personalized support limited to select clients," "data-driven and results-oriented," "transparent, value-focused methodology."** The "limited to select clients" line is the scarcity/selectivity play; it substitutes for explicit pricing or availability. No handling of the usual enterprise objections (how do you work with in-house teams, what about reporting cadence, how long are engagements, what does week 1 look like).

## Design system observations

- **Type:** clean sans-serif throughout. No serif accent, no display face, no editorial typographic texture.
- **Color:** white background, grayscale imagery, single blue accent on CTAs and links. Conservative — reads "trustworthy SaaS vendor" more than "senior operator."
- **Motion:** essentially static. No scroll-triggered reveals, no hover state beyond default link color. Quiet page.
- **Density:** airy. Generous whitespace, card-based service grid, single-column testimonials. Low information density per scroll.
- **Structural signal:** page reads as a seven-block vertical stack (hero → intro → services → about → why → testimonials → contact). No sticky nav elaboration, no pricing page, no case studies page, no blog on this domain (content lives on aleydasolis.com).

## What we'd steal

- **Named-human testimonials over logo reel as the hero proof.** Orainti leads with *"extension of my internal team"* (Matt Hurn, Turo) — exactly the frame the "embedded senior operator" pillar needs. For a solo consultant, a named VP saying "extension of my team" is stronger than a logo you could have worked with once. We should pull 2–3 short, relational quotes onto the resonanceseo.com hero area, each tagged with full name + title + company. Our advantage: we can do this *and* show Adidas/Samsung/J.Crew/Madewell as the logo cluster underneath, which Orainti cannot.

- **"Select clients" scarcity as positioning, not as marketing gimmick.** Orainti's *"personalized support limited to select clients"* says "we are senior, we do not scale horizontally" without ever using the word "senior." It reframes low-throughput as the feature. This is the exact posture resonanceseo.com's "Hours Available This Month: X of 160" bar should carry — the bar is better (it's honest, numeric, operator-updated) but the narrative framing around it should borrow Orainti's tone: selectivity as a promise, not a limitation.

- **Anti-agency framing via operator-voice testimonials.** Every Orainti testimonial reads as "I worked directly with her," never "they ran a campaign for us." That direct-access language does the anti-agency work without a "we're not an agency" disclaimer section. We should screen our testimonial shortlist for quotes that contain first-person-plural work language ("we worked together," "she embedded with our team") and cut anything that sounds like a vendor-review.

## What we'd leave

- **The "How can Orainti help you?" provocative-question H1.** Wrong for our ICP. A VP of E-commerce at $50M+ has already decided she needs help; she wants to verify fit in 3 seconds. A question H1 defers the positioning claim to the subhead and makes the reader do the work. Our locked H1 draft *"Senior SEO + AI search leadership for enterprise e-commerce — embedded directly in your team"* does the positioning in one line — position-first beats question-first for this buyer. Keep our draft, leave Orainti's pattern.

- **The "boutique consultancy" framing and no-pricing posture.** Orainti keeps prices off the site and leans on "boutique" language. We made the opposite call: published tiers ($6,400 / $9,600 / $12,800) with a real availability bar. For our positioning — *honest solo operator, no agency markup* — visible pricing *is* the anti-agency proof. Hiding pricing would contradict our anti-positioning pillar #1. Leave the boutique framing; keep published tiers.

- **The single-CTA "GET IN TOUCH" everywhere.** Orainti runs one CTA label into one contact form across the whole page. That collapses the funnel and suits a consultant who's fine with full-context inbound only. Our two-layer strategy — newsletter primary, strategy call secondary — is deliberately staged for a buyer who may not be ready to book a call on first visit. The "GET IN TOUCH" monomania would lose the top-of-funnel capture we need for the GEO Readiness Framework path. Leave it.

- **Zero-motion, grayscale-imagery design posture.** Orainti's aesthetic is quiet to the point of forgettable — there's nothing in the typography, color, or motion that signals "2026" or "AI search capability." Our GEO-capable positioning (pillar #3) needs a design system with at least one contemporary signal (editorial type pairing, considered motion, or a typographic moment) that separates us from yesterday's-SEO-consultant visual defaults. Not bold for bold's sake — but not Orainti-quiet either.
