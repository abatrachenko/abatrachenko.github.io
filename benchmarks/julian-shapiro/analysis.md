# Julian Shapiro — julian.com

**URL:** https://www.julian.com
**Captured:** 2026-04-23
**Category:** Solo-expert (investor + writer + former growth operator)

## Why this site

Julian Shapiro's personal site is a widely-imitated template for a senior solo operator's marketing presence. It solves the problem resonanceseo.com has: how does a named individual signal credibility to sophisticated buyers without sounding like a LinkedIn brochure? Julian does it by refusing to sell. The site is a body of work — four "handbooks," a blog, a podcast — with a single-line investment pitch as the hero and a terse bio at the bottom. Proof is implied by the artifacts, not asserted. Directly relevant to a solo SEO consultant selling $6.4K–$12.8K/mo engagements to enterprise buyers suspicious of self-promotion.

## Hero

- **Headline approach:** direct question-to-prospect. The first line a visitor sees is **"I invest in deeptech startups. Are you fundraising for one?"** — both position statement and filter. It names what he does and qualifies the reader in one sentence.
- **Subhead strategy:** no separate subhead. The page title underneath is simply **"The Handbooks Of Julian Shapiro,"** which acts as a content-hub header rather than a benefit line.
- **CTA:** no button in the hero. No "Book a call." No "Get in touch." The CTA is the sentence itself — if you're fundraising, you reach out; if not, you scroll.
- **Supporting visual:** none. No portrait, no hero image. The page opens on text.

## Proof

- **Social proof format:** named-drop embedded in the About paragraph, not logo-wall. The proof lands in one long sentence at the bottom of the page: *"Previously, I ran growth for startups including Webflow—and I created Velocity, an animation engine used by Samsung, Uber, WhatsApp, and thousands more. Velocity was awarded the Stripe Open Source Grant."*
- **Placement:** bottom, after the handbooks and blog posts. The body of work is the proof; the bio confirms it.
- **What makes it credible:** specifics. Named companies (Samsung, Uber, WhatsApp, Webflow, Stripe), a named artifact (Velocity), a named award (Stripe Open Source Grant). No adjectives. "Two million words" anchors the Writing handbook without ever being hoisted into the hero.

## Offer structure

- **How he packages what he sells:** he doesn't. The only commercial offer on the page is one sentence: *"I invest in deeptech startups via Julian.capital."* No services, no retainers, no coaching, no course. The four handbooks are free.
- **Pricing visibility:** none. Nothing to price — the site is not transactional. This is the key structural difference from what resonanceseo.com must be.

## FAQ / objections

- No FAQ on the homepage. Julian sidesteps objection-handling by not asking for anything. The only filter is the hero sentence, which self-qualifies — if you aren't a fundraising deeptech founder, the page isn't for you and he's fine with that.

## Design system observations

- **Type:** sans-serif throughout, body and display. No serif accents. Moderate size contrast — handbook titles are large but not dramatic.
- **Color:** dark mode by default. Background **#1e242e** (desaturated near-black navy), text **#e6e6e6** (warm off-white). Near-zero accent color; links carry a subtle hue but nothing shouts.
- **Motion:** minimal. No scroll-triggered animation, no hero motion. Notable, given Julian built Velocity — he declines to perform the capability.
- **Density:** airy. Single-column, generous line-height, meaningful whitespace between sections. Handbook cards sit as a quiet grid with small SVG icons (speaker, pen, rocket, dumbbell) rather than photography.

**Information architecture, top to bottom:**
1. Hero line: "I invest in deeptech startups. Are you fundraising for one?"
2. "The Handbooks Of Julian Shapiro" — four cards: *HiFi Audio* ("Some speakers play music in ways you've never heard."), *Writing Well* ("After two million words, I've honed these writing tactics."), *Start a Startup* ("How to identify great startup ideas and acquire users."), *Build Muscle* ("The actual science of building muscle.")
3. Brains Podcast (single card, "Listen →")
4. Blog Posts — seven essay titles ("What to do with your life," "How to tell great stories," "What are mental models?")
5. About Julian — the bio paragraph, closing with a list of personal likes and dislikes

## What we'd steal

- **A. Hero as filter, not pitch.** Julian opens with a sentence that names his work and qualifies the reader in one move. For resonanceseo.com this maps onto the locked H1: *"Senior SEO + AI search leadership for enterprise e-commerce — embedded directly in your team."* Same job — names the work, names the buyer, filters out sub-$50M brands. No button needed in the first viewport; the sentence is the filter. This is also why Julian's hero lands without a portrait: **credibility in the first viewport is carried by the specificity of the language, not by a photograph of a person smiling**. Resist the impulse to hero-shot Aleksey.

- **B. Proof-by-artifact, not proof-by-claim.** Julian never writes "I'm a world-class writer" — he puts a 500-hour writing handbook on the homepage and lets you click it. The handbooks *are* the proof. For resonanceseo.com this argues for leading with case studies (Adidas +240K daily clicks, J.Crew +95%, Alchemy +315%) rendered as clickable cards, not as a "Results" bullet list. The case-study card is to resonanceseo what the handbook card is to julian.com: a tangible artifact that makes the claim unnecessary.

- **C. Bio as *final* proof, not opening.** Julian's credentials (Webflow, Samsung/Uber/WhatsApp via Velocity, Stripe grant) land at the *bottom* of the page — after the reader has already been shown the work. This sequencing (artifact → outcomes → offer → bio) inverts the conventional consultant site (hero → logos → bio → services). For resonanceseo.com: H1 → case-study cards → offer tiers → bio with $700M/Adidas/Samsung/HBS context. The bio closes the loop; it doesn't open the pitch.

- **D. One-line descriptions under every card.** Each handbook gets a single sentence underneath — no paragraph, no bullets. That density of meaning per line is the voice target for resonanceseo's case-study card descriptions and section subheads. Every sentence earns its line.

- **E. Desaturated palette carrying tier signal.** The #1e242e / #e6e6e6 combination reads premium and editorial without a single "design gesture" — no gradients, no glow, no accent pops. Whitespace does the tier work. Resonance should audit its color system for any color or gradient that exists only to "look designed."

## What we'd leave

- **No commercial CTA anywhere.** Julian can afford this because he isn't running a consulting practice — he invests, and deal flow is inbound from a small qualified pool. Resonance is a consulting practice with a booked-hours scarcity signal and a 3-month minimum; it needs the two-layer CTA (newsletter primary, strategy-call secondary). Inherit his restraint on CTA *frequency* (2–3 placements, not 8) — not his absence of CTA.

- **Personal likes/dislikes list in the bio.** Julian closes with *"Things I enjoy include Wild Beasts, Counterpart, Denis Villeneuve, Frank Ocean, Kobe Bryant, and KFBR392."* It works because the site is a personal presence, not a pitch. For an enterprise buyer deciding on a $10K/mo engagement, that register flips from charming to unserious. Keep the bio warm; drop the cultural-signal list.

- **Four-equal-handbooks framing.** Julian presents four handbooks as peers; none is his primary revenue driver because none is revenue. Resonance has a single locked offer (fractional SEO+GEO leadership, three tiers). Avoid presenting Reach LLM advisory, writing, and speaking as peer offerings alongside the consulting. The consulting is the offer; everything else is credential.

- **Homepage as content hub rather than offer page.** Julian's homepage essentially *is* a blog index. For resonanceseo.com the newsletter and any future content library belong one click deeper; the homepage has to convert on positioning + proof + offer. Steal Julian's restraint, not his hub-first IA.
